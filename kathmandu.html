Kathmandu

Boudhanath Stupa

Boudhanath Stupa, one of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an important center for Tibetan Buddhism. With its massive white dome and golden spire, it serves as a sacred place for meditation and prayer. Surrounding the stupa are monasteries, shops, and restaurants, making it a vibrant cultural hub.

Cost: $50

Kathmandu Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square is a historic royal palace complex that showcases Nepalese architecture and culture. This UNESCO World Heritage Site features stunning temples, courtyards, and statues that date back to the Malla period. Visitors can explore the Hanuman Dhoka Palace, Kumari Ghar (home of the living goddess), and many ancient shrines.

Cost: $40

Pashupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath Temple is one of the most revered Hindu temples in Nepal, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated on the banks of the Bagmati River, it is famous for its spiritual significance and traditional cremation ceremonies. The temple complex includes many shrines, ashrams, and sculptures that attract thousands of pilgrims and tourists each year.

Cost: $60
